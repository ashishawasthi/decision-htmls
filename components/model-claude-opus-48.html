Claude Opus 4.8 in agentic systems

A premium model for agentic workflows, coding, and careful tool use, available on Vertex AI alongside the Gemini models. It is the most expensive option here. The design rule that follows: use it where its agentic and instruction-following strength earns the price, and as a different model for cross-checking outputs.

When to pick it

Good fit

  • Complex agentic workflows, coding, and long chains of careful tool calls.
  • Cross-model judge diversity: a different vendor checks or judges the primary model's output.
  • Tasks where instruction-following and reliability justify the premium price.

Avoid

  • Cost-sensitive, high-volume traffic: the per-token price is the highest here.
  • Sub-second SLOs where time to first token must stay small.

How it maps to this tool

  • Reasoning model for agentic or coding-heavy designs. It carries the reasoning share of routed traffic; its high output price is what lifts the cost panel when selected.
  • Integrated search tool: yes. A web search tool, so live web grounding runs without a warning, billed about $10 per 1k searches.
  • Cross-model judge diversity. Turning on the Cross-model judge diversity lever pairs it with a Gemini primary, so a different vendor reviews the answer.
